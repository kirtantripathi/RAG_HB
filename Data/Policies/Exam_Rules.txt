Examination Rules and Regulations  
Academic Year: 2025–2026  

---------------------------------------------------------
1. Eligibility for Appearing in Examinations
---------------------------------------------------------
- A student must have:
  ✔ Minimum **75% attendance** in each course.  
  ✔ Completed all internal assignments, lab submissions, and project work.  
  ✔ No pending disciplinary actions.

- Students failing criteria will be **barred from exams** until compliance.

---------------------------------------------------------
2. Types of Examinations
---------------------------------------------------------
- **Internal Assessment (IA):**  
  - 20–30% weightage depending on course.  
  - Includes quizzes, assignments, viva, and mid-semester tests.

- **Mid-Semester Examination:**  
  - Scheduled in Week 7 or 8 of the semester.  

- **End-Semester Examination (ESE):**  
  - Final graded examination covering full syllabus.  
  - Weightage: 40–60%.

---------------------------------------------------------
3. Exam Hall Rules
---------------------------------------------------------
- Students must reach the examination hall **at least 15 minutes early**.
- Entry will **not be permitted** after the first **20 minutes** of exam start time.
- Students must carry:
  ✔ College ID card  
  ✔ Valid hall ticket  
  ✔ Required stationery  

- Mobile phones, smartwatches, calculators with storage, or any electronic devices are strictly prohibited unless permitted by syllabus.

---------------------------------------------------------
4. Seating and Identification
---------------------------------------------------------
- Seating will be assigned by **roll number or seat chart**.
- Students must sign the **attendance sheet**.
- Invigilators have authority to verify ID or seating at any time.

---------------------------------------------------------
5. Conduct During Examination
---------------------------------------------------------
- Silence must be maintained at all times.
- Students may raise hand to seek invigilator assistance.
- No student may leave the hall during the first **45 minutes**.
- Answers must be written only in **official answer sheets** provided.

---------------------------------------------------------
6. Cheating and Malpractice
---------------------------------------------------------
The following are considered academic misconduct:
- Communicating with another student.
- Possessing unauthorized notes or digital devices.
- Copying, impersonation, or assisting others.

Penalties may include:
- Cancellation of exam paper  
- Suspension for current semester  
- Expulsion in severe cases  
- Permanent disciplinary record  

---------------------------------------------------------
7. Re-Exam and Backlog Policy
---------------------------------------------------------
- Students failing a course may appear for the **Supplementary Examination** held next semester.
- A maximum of **four backlogs** allowed to progress to next academic year.
- Improvement exams are allowed only for **End-Semester Exams** once per course.

---------------------------------------------------------
8. Result and Revaluation
---------------------------------------------------------
- Results will be declared on the **Student Academic Portal**.
- Students may apply for:
  ✔ Revaluation  
  ✔ Photocopy of answer script  
  ✔ Rechecking of marks  

- Requests must be submitted within **7 working days** after results.

---------------------------------------------------------
9. Special Exam Accommodations
---------------------------------------------------------
Students with disabilities may request:
- Extra time
- Assistive writer
- Accessible seating
with valid documentation approved by Academic Council.

---------------------------------------------------------
10. General Disclaimer
---------------------------------------------------------
- The examination committee reserves the right to update, interpret, or modify rules as required.
- Students must check official notifications regularly.

---------------------------------------------------------
Note: Ignorance of rules will not be considered as an excuse.
